## Setup

- **Model**: Qwen3-0.6B-Base, weight-tied embeddings
- **Tokenizer**: HF `tokenizers` 0.21.1; `huggingface_hub` 0.30.2; `torch` 2.6.0
- **Sampling**: multinomial from softmax (post-fix; see below)
- **Prompts**: 30 fixed `test_prompts` (encyclopedia / synthesis style)
- **Hardware**: 8× GPUs, one job per GPU, 60 jobs total per run (30 prompts × {WM, UW})
- **PRC**: LDPC-PRC₀ (zero-bit), keys auto-generated by `KeyGen(n, t, g, noise_rate, …)` from `prc.py`
- **Test statistic**: `Σ log_plus − ½ Σ (log_plus + log_minus)` over r parity checks where `Πᵢ = ∏ⱼ (1−2η)(1−2·OTPⱼ)·postⱼ` for the t slots in row i
- **Threshold rule**: `threshold = null_mean + Φ⁻¹(1−FPR)·null_std`, null fit from 2000 simulated null draws (Bernoulli channel applied to uniform random codewords using actual watermarked p-traces)

## The argmax → multinomial fix

Initial generation used `argmax` after the partition mask. This deterministically picks the highest-logit token within whichever half the watermark bit `b` selects. **It destroys the watermark signal** because the conditional probability of each codeword bit collapses to {0, 1} independent of `b`.

Result with argmax: TPR = 0/30, FPR = 0/30 at every threshold tested.

**Fix** (in `watermark_expt.py:265-279`): sample from softmax of the masked logits with `torch.multinomial`. This restores the proper PRC sampling distribution.

Result after fix: TPR jumps from 0% to 96.7% at FPR=1e-9 with n=4096, t=3.

This is the single largest finding of the experimental work.

## Entropy vs naive fold

Entropy fold downweights each posterior by its local Shannon entropy `H₂(p₁)/ln 2 ∈ [0, 1]`. Two effects, both visible in the data:

1. **Suppress corrupted parity checks** (smaller effect, ~10% of swing). A check that touches a low-entropy slot has a near-deterministic ±1 posterior at that slot, making Π ≈ random ±|signal|. Entropy weighting drives such posteriors → 0, collapsing Π → 0 and silencing the check.

2. **Clean up surviving checks at the *observation* level** (bigger effect, ~90% of swing). Even slots that look "clean" by mean-weight have individual deterministic observations dragging the slot's naive posterior toward random ±1. Entropy weighting downweights each individual deterministic observation, so each slot's posterior is dominated by high-entropy observations whose signs actually correlate with the codeword.

**Quantitative gap (entropy − naive TPR at FPR=1e-9):**

| config | gap |
|---|---|
| n=4096, t=14 | +5/30 |
| n=4096, t=6 g=144 | +7/30 |
| n=128, t=3 | +1/30 (sweep), +1 to +6 across other FPR targets |
| n=128, t=7 | +2/30 |

Entropy fold wins at every FPR target across every config.

## Outlier prompt analysis (n=128, t=3)

Worst-case watermarked prompt under naive fold: **prompt index 23** (encyclopedia of solar system bodies). Naive stat = −0.239 (below null mean!), entropy stat = +4.599.

**Statistic decomposition (sum over r=91 parity checks):**

| fold | total | corrupted (13/91) | clean (78/91) |
|---|---|---|---|
| naive | −0.239 | +0.057 | −0.296 |
| entropy | +4.599 | +0.554 | +4.044 |

A check is "corrupted" here iff at least one of its t=3 slots has mean per-token entropy weight < 0.05.

- 5 of 128 slots are low-entropy
- 13 of 91 checks touch ≥1 low-entropy slot
- Naive: 13 corrupted checks net out to +0.057 (random cancellation), 78 clean checks net to −0.296 (dragged toward zero by per-observation determinism)
- Entropy: corrupted checks silenced to +0.554, clean checks contribute +4.044

The +4.34-point swing on clean checks dominates — most of the recovery happens at the *observation* level inside individual slots, not at the *check* level. Per-check filtering ("drop the corrupted ones") is the smaller of the two effects.

**Demo data**: `outlier_demo.json` carries:
- `per_token` (1024 entries): `i, slot, token_id, token_str, p1, weight, bit, low_entropy`
- `per_slot` (128 entries): `n_obs, mean_weight, naive_posterior, entropy_posterior, low_entropy`
- `per_check` (91 entries): `slot_indices, Pi_naive, Pi_entropy, contrib_naive, contrib_entropy, corrupted`

## FPR sweep methodology

`fpr_sweep.py` (entropy) / `fpr_sweep_naive.py` (naive) take saved p-traces from `calib_workdir/` and:

1. Refit null distribution (2000 sims) using the appropriate fold on the watermarked p-traces. Each null draw: pick a random WM p-trace, draw a uniform random codeword, push through the Bernoulli channel `bern_p = where(p ≤ 0.5, 2·xi·p, 1 − 2·(1−xi)·(1−p))`, sample binary observations, fold to posteriors, compute statistic.
2. For each FPR target, set `threshold = null_mean + Φ⁻¹(1−FPR)·null_std`.
3. Compute TPR = #{WM stats > threshold} / 30.
4. Compute empirical FPR = #{UW stats > threshold} / 30.

**Why empirical FPR is 0/30 everywhere**: the simulated null is built from watermarked p-traces (deliberate — to model the strongest adversary distribution) but real unwatermarked sampling produces tighter UW stats than the simulated null suggests. The Gaussian-tail extrapolation is therefore conservative on this benchmark.

**Sample-size limit**: 30 unwatermarked samples cannot *prove* an FPR below ~3.3% even when empirical is 0. To certify FPR=1e-9 you need either the Gaussian-tail simulation (used here) or a much larger UW sample.

## Headline takeaways

1. **argmax-after-mask was the single biggest bug** — TPR went 0% → 97% by switching to multinomial.
2. **Theorem-2-aligned config (t=½log₂n, g=log²n, η=0.05) gives the best operating point**: 97% TPR @ FPR=1e-9 at n=4096 *with* proper pseudorandomness.
3. **Entropy fold beats naive at every (n, t, FPR target)** — gap is 5–7 prompts at FPR=1e-9 for n=4096; smaller but consistent at n=128.
4. **Most of the entropy-fold benefit is at the observation level**, not the parity-check level. The "drop corrupted checks" framing captures only ~10% of the swing.
5. **Empirical FPR is 0/30 everywhere** — the simulated-null Gaussian-tail extrapolation is conservative on this benchmark. With only 30 UW samples we can't certify FPR < ~3% empirically, only via the simulation.
6. **Small n (128) is fundamentally signal-limited** — fewer parity checks cap test-statistic dynamic range regardless of how many codeword repeats are stacked.
